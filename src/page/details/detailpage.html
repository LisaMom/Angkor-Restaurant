Siem Reap

Temple Kingdom

Ancient wonders, culture, and magical sunrise moments.

Siem Reap: The Gateway to Antiquity Siem Reap is the cultural heartbeat of Cambodia, where the grandeur of the ancient Khmer Empire meets a bustling, modern hospitality scene. As home to the legendary Angkor Archaeological Park, it offers a journey back in time through towering stone faces and intricate bas-reliefs. Beyond the temples, the province charms visitors with its floating villages on the Tonle Sap, vibrant night markets, and a world-class dining scene. It is a place of profound spirituality and artistic heritage, perfect for those seeking both inspiration and adventure.

Open: 5:00AM -> 5:30 PM

Uniqueness: The world's largest religious monument.

Kulen Mountain

Uniqueness: Birthplace of the Khmer Empire with underwater carvings.

Phnom Penh

Capital Energy

History, skyline, food, and city lifestyle.

Phnom Penh: The Pearl of Asia Phnom Penh is a captivating city of contrasts, where gold-roofed pagodas sit alongside sleek skyscrapers and French colonial villas. Located at the "Chaktomuk" (the four faces) confluence of the Mekong and Tonle Sap rivers, the capital is a hub of energy, history, and resilience. From the sobering history of Tuol Sleng to the regal elegance of the Royal Palace, the city tells the story of a nation that has overcome its past to embrace a vibrant future. It is the best place to experience Cambodia’s urban evolution, riverside strolls, and sophisticated nightlife.

Royal Palace

Location: Sothearos Blvd, Phnom Penh

Open: 8:00 AM – 11:00 AM / 2:00 PM – 5:00 PM

Uniqueness: The official residence of the King of Cambodia and a stunning example of Khmer architecture.

Tuol Sleng Museum (S-21)

Location : Sangkat BKK3

Open: 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM

Uniqueness: Preserved former high school turned secret prison.

National Museum

Location : Next to the Royal Palace

Open: 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM

Uniqueness: The finest collection of ancient Khmer sculpture.

Sihanoukville

Sea Escape

Island vibes, beaches, and sunset adventures.

Sihanoukville: The Coastal Frontier Once a quiet port town, Sihanoukville has transformed into a dynamic gateway to Cambodia’s most stunning tropical treasures. While the city itself is a fast-growing hub, its true magic lies in the surrounding coastline and the pristine islands of the Koh Rong Archipelago. With its turquoise waters, powdery white sands, and lush national parks like Ream, it is a haven for sun-seekers and water sports enthusiasts. Whether you want to dance at a beach party or find a secluded bay to watch the sunset, this province offers the ultimate seaside escape.

Otres Beach

Open: 24/7

Uniqueness: A famous, laid-back bohemian atmosphere.

Ratanakiri

Wild Nature

Lakes, forests, waterfalls, and adventure.

Ratanakiri: The Wild Highland For the traveler looking to go off the beaten path, Ratanakiri offers a rugged landscape of red dirt roads, ancient volcanic lakes, and dense primary rainforests. Located in the remote northeast, this "gem-stone" province is famous for its natural wonders like the emerald Yeak Laom Lake and its rich indigenous culture. It is a land of cascading waterfalls and rare wildlife, providing a raw and authentic experience of Cambodia’s hinterland. Ratanakiri is the perfect destination for trekkers, nature lovers, and those wanting to connect with the traditions of the highland tribes.

Yeak Laom Lake

Uniqueness: A 4,000-year-old volcanic crater lake.

Uniqueness: Surrounded by rubber plantations and suspension bridges.

Virachey National Park

Location : Border of Laos/Vietnam

Open: Best with a guide (Day trips)

Uniqueness: One of the largest protected wildernesses in Cambodia.

Kep

Coastal Charm

Fresh seafood, sea breeze, and peaceful views.

Kep: The Elegant Retreat Kep is Cambodia’s most tranquil seaside destination, known for its nostalgic charm and world-famous seafood. Originally founded as a prestigious retreat for the French elite, the town is dotted with hauntingly beautiful colonial ruins and lush greenery. It offers a slower pace of life where the main "event" is choosing a fresh crab at the Crab Market or taking a short boat ride to the rustic Rabbit Island. With its quiet beaches and scenic mountain trails, Kep is the ideal sanctuary for those who want to disconnect and rejuvenate by the sea.

Kep Beach

Open: 24/7

Uniqueness: Calm crescent beach flanked by seafood shacks.

The Crab Market

Uniqueness: Watch live crabs pulled directly from the ocean.

Kep National Park

Open: Dawn to Dusk

Uniqueness: An 8km jungle loop with panoramic ocean views.

Kampot

Riverside Beauty

Mountains, pepper farms, and calm rivers.

Kampot: The Riverside Sanctuary Kampot is a province that invites you to slow down and breathe. Nestled along the banks of the Praek Tuek Chhu River and framed by the misty Elephant Mountains, it is famous for producing the world’s best pepper and being a center of salt production. The town is a masterpiece of colonial architecture, now filled with cozy cafes and boutique stays. From the eerie heights of Bokor Hill to the glowing fireflies on the river at night, Kampot offers a perfect blend of mountain air, riverside bliss, and rural beauty.

Uniqueness: An eerie, mist-covered French colonial ghost town.

Kampot Pepper Farm

Battambang

Cultural Heartbeat

Colonial charm, the unique Bamboo Train, and ancient cave formations.

Battambang: Battambang is Cambodia’s artistic sanctuary and the nation’s legendary "Rice Bowl." This northwest province offers a sophisticated yet laid-back charm, featuring some of the best-preserved French colonial architecture in Southeast Asia. Recently recognized as a UNESCO Creative City of Gastronomy, it is a paradise for foodies and culture seekers alike. From the quirky thrill of the Bamboo Train to the haunting beauty of the Bat Caves at sunset, Battambang provides a deep, authentic connection to Khmer history and modern creativity that feels a world away from the crowded tourist hubs.

The Bamboo Train (Norry)

Location: 4km Southeast of the city center

Open: 8:00 AM – 5:30 PM

Uniqueness: A DIY rail vehicle made of bamboo and small engines—truly one-of-a-kind.

Phnom Sampeau (Bat Caves)

Open: 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM

Uniqueness: Refreshing jungle pools perfect for a hot day.

Phare Ponleu Selpak

Open: Evening shows (Usually 7:00 PM)

Uniqueness: A world-renowned NGO circus school that blends acrobatics, theater, and music.

Kampong Cham

Riverside Heritage

Scenic river views, unique bamboo architecture, and ancient hilltop temples.

Kampong Cham: Kampong Cham is a province where the rhythm of life is set by the gentle flow of the Mekong River. Known as the "Gateway to the East," it is a place of rustic charm, featuring wide riverside boulevards, peeling French colonial buildings, and a warm, welcoming atmosphere. As one of Cambodia's most fertile regions, it is famous for its vast rubber plantations and tobacco fields. Whether you are crossing a handcrafted bamboo bridge or exploring ancient temples that blend the old with the new, Kampong Cham offers an authentic slice of Cambodian life that feels both timeless and serene.

Koh Paen Bamboo Bridge

Location: Mekong River bank, Kampong Cham Town

Open: 6:00 AM – 6:00 PM (Best in dry season: Nov–May)

Uniqueness: A world-famous "living" bridge built entirely by hand using thousands of bamboo poles. It is torn down and rebuilt every single year!

Phnom Srey & Phnom Pros

Open: 7:00 AM – 6:00 PM

Uniqueness: Two legendary hills named after a competition between men and women to see who could build the highest mountain by dawn. (The women won!)

Wat Nokor Bachey

Location : 2.2km from the city center

Open: 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM

Uniqueness: A fascinating "temple within a temple" where a modern 19th-century pagoda is built inside 11th-century Angkorian ruins.

Preah Vihear

The Temple in the Clouds

Stunning cliff-top views, ancient stairways, and a unique north-south temple axis.

Preah Vihear: Preah Vihear is the crown jewel of Cambodia’s northern highlands, a province where ancient stone meets the edge of the sky. Named after its world-famous mountain temple, this rugged region is defined by the dramatic Dângrêk Mountains and vast, untouched forests. It is a destination for the true explorer, offering a quiet majesty that feels distant from the modern world. Home to two UNESCO World Heritage sites—the cliff-top Preah Vihear Temple and the mysterious pyramid of Koh Ker—the province is a testament to the architectural daring of the Khmer Empire. It is a land of sweeping horizons, emerald jungles, and deep spiritual significance.

Preah Vihear Temple

Location: Atop Poy Tadi Cliff, Dângrêk Mountains

Open: 7:00 AM – 5:00 PM

Uniqueness: A UNESCO World Heritage site perched 525 meters above the plains; built along a unusual north-south axis.

Koh Ker Archaeological Site

Location : Srayang Commune, Kuleaen District

Open: 7:00 AM – 5:30 PM

Uniqueness: Home to Prasat Prang, a 35-meter-high, seven-stepped pyramid that looks more like a Mayan temple than a typical Khmer ruin.

Preah Khan of Kampong Svay (Ba Kan)

Location : Sangkum Thmei District

Open: 7:00 AM – 5:00 PM

Uniqueness: One of the largest temple complexes ever built during the Angkorian era, once a major military and industrial outpost.

Takeo

Cradle of Civilization

Ancient pre-Angkorian ruins, scenic mountain climbs, and a sprawling wildlife sanctuary.

Takeo: Takeo is widely celebrated as the "cradle of Khmer civilization," offering a journey into the deepest roots of Cambodian history. Located in the fertile south, just a short drive from Phnom Penh, this peaceful province is home to some of the country's oldest archaeological treasures, dating back to the pre-Angkorian Funan and Chenla eras. Takeo is a beautiful tapestry of flooded plains, ancient canals, and sacred hills. It is a destination where you can escape the tourist crowds and explore pristine ancient temples that sit quietly on mountaintops, making it a perfect spot for history lovers and weekend explorers alike.

Phnom Chisor

Location: 27km North of Takeo Town

Open: 7:00 AM – 5:00 PM

Uniqueness: An 11th-century mountain temple reachable by climbing 503 steps. It offers breathtaking, panoramic views of endless rice paddies.

Tonle Bati & Ta Prohm Temple

Open: 7:00 AM – 6:00 PM

Uniqueness: A beautiful lake dotted with over-water bamboo shacks for picnicking, right next to a charming, well-preserved 12th-century Angkorian temple.

Banteay Meanchey

The Silent Sentinel

Massive stone face-towers, intricate bas-reliefs, and rare crane sanctuaries.

Banteay Meanchey: Banteay Meanchey is Cambodia’s northwestern "Fortress of Victory," a province that blends high-energy border commerce with profound historical serenity. While many travelers know it for the bustling gateway of Poipet, its true soul lies in the silent, moss-covered stones of Banteay Chhmar, a temple complex that rivals Angkor in beauty but without the crowds. It is a land of vast wetlands and golden rice fields, home to rare wildlife and ancient legends of military triumph. For those looking to escape the modern world, this province offers a raw, authentic connection to the Khmer Empire’s enduring spirit.

Banteay Chhmar Temple

Location: Thma Puok District (60km north of Sisophon)

Open: 7:30 AM – 5:30 PM

Uniqueness: One of the few temples featuring the "enigmatic smiles" of Bayon-style face-towers and a massive wall of multi-armed Avalokitesvara.

Ang Trapeang Thmor Sanctuary

Location : Phnom Srok District

Open: Best visited at dawn (5:30 AM – 8:00 AM)

Uniqueness: A critical wetland habitat for the endangered Sarus Crane and the rare Eld's Deer.

Phnom Bak & Phnom Chenh Chung

Location : Serei Saophoan City (Sisophon)

Open: 24/7 (Best for sunrise/sunset)

Uniqueness: Twin limestone mountains offering a 360-degree view of the province and a colorful hilltop pagoda.

Siem Reap

FunFact : The name "Siem Reap" literally translates to "Siam Defeated," commemorating a 16th-century Khmer victory over the Thai (Siam) army. It’s also home to the world’s largest religious monument—Angkor Wat!

Phnom Penh

FunFact : The city was named after a wealthy woman named Lady Penh. Legend says she found four Buddha statues in a floating tree and built a hill (Phnom) and a temple (Wat Phnom) to house them.

Sihanouk Ville

FunFact : The city was built in the 1950s as Cambodia’s only deep-sea port. It was named after King Norodom Sihanouk, who is fondly remembered as the "Father of Independence."

Rattanakiri

FunFact : The name comes from the Sanskrit words Ratna (Gem) and Kiri (Mountain). It is literally the "Gem Mountain" province, famous for its rare blue zircons and volcanic soil.

Kep

FunFact : Kep is the smallest province in Cambodia! It was once the most prestigious beach resort for the French and Khmer elite, earning the nickname "The Saint-Tropez of Southeast Asia."

Kompot

FunFact : Kampot Pepper was the first Cambodian product to receive "Geographical Indication" (GI) status—the same protection given to Champagne in France. It is widely considered by top chefs to be the best pepper in the world!

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before coming to Cambodia.

Do I need a visa to visit Cambodia?

Most travelers need a visa. If you're from an ASEAN country, you can enter visa-free for 14–30 days. For everyone else, you can easily apply for an E-Visa online ($36) or get a Visa on Arrival ($30) at the airport.
Pro Tip: As of 2026, don't forget to complete your e-Arrival Card online within 7 days before you land!

What currency should I bring?

Cambodia uses a unique dual-currency system: US Dollars (USD) and Cambodian Riel (KHR). Both are accepted everywhere!
$1 is roughly equal to 4,000 Riel. You’ll often pay in dollars and receive your "small change" back in Riel.
Pro Tip: Make sure your US bills are crisp and clean—banks and shops may not accept torn or old notes.

How do I get around the cities?

The easiest and most fun way is by Tuk-Tuk! To get the best price without haggling, download the Grab or PassApp mobile apps.
Most short rides in the city cost between $1 and $3. It’s safe, fast, and gives you a front-row seat to the local life.

Is there a dress code for the temples?

Yes! Cambodia is a deeply respectful culture. When visiting sacred sites like Angkor Wat or the Royal Palace, you must cover your shoulders and knees.
Note: Wearing a shawl or scarf over a tank top is sometimes rejected by guards—it’s best to wear a T-shirt and long pants or a long skirt to ensure entry.
