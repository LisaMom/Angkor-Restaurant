Siem Reap

Temple Kingdom

Ancient wonders, culture, and magical sunrise moments.

Siem Reap: The Gateway to Antiquity
Siem Reap is the cultural heartbeat of Cambodia, where the grandeur of the ancient Khmer Empire meets a bustling, modern hospitality scene. As home to the legendary Angkor Archaeological Park, it offers a journey back in time through towering stone faces and intricate bas-reliefs. Beyond the temples, the province charms visitors with floating villages on the Tonle Sap, vibrant night markets, and a world-class dining scene.

Angkor Sunrise

Location: 5km North of Siem Reap

Open: 5:00 AM – 5:30 PM

Uniqueness: The world's largest religious monument.

Preah Dak Villages

Location: Near East Baray

Open: 6:00 AM – 6:00 PM

Uniqueness: Famous for handmade palm sugar and Khmer noodles.

Kulen Mountain

Location: 50km North of the city

Open: 7:00 AM – 5:00 PM

Uniqueness: Birthplace of the Khmer Empire with underwater carvings.

Phnom Penh

Capital Energy

History, skyline, food, and city lifestyle.

Phnom Penh: The Pearl of Asia
Phnom Penh is a captivating city of contrasts, where gold-roofed pagodas sit alongside sleek skyscrapers and French colonial villas. Located at the "Chaktomuk" confluence of the Mekong and Tonle Sap rivers, the capital is a hub of energy, history, and resilience. From Tuol Sleng to the Royal Palace, it tells the story of a nation that has overcome its past to embrace a vibrant future.

Royal Palace

Location: Sothearos Blvd, Phnom Penh

Open: 8:00 AM – 11:00 AM / 2:00 PM – 5:00 PM

Uniqueness: Official residence of the King of Cambodia.

Tuol Sleng Museum (S-21)

Location: Sangkat BKK3

Open: 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM

Uniqueness: Preserved former high school turned secret prison.

National Museum

Location: Next to the Royal Palace

Open: 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM

Uniqueness: The finest collection of ancient Khmer sculpture.

Sihanoukville: The Coastal Frontier
Once a quiet port town, Sihanoukville has transformed into a dynamic gateway to Cambodia's most stunning tropical treasures. Its true magic lies in the Koh Rong Archipelago — turquoise waters, powdery white sands, and lush national parks like Ream.

Koh Rong

Location: 25km off the coast

Open: Ferries 8:00 AM – 4:00 PM

Uniqueness: Powder-white sands and glowing night plankton.

Ream National Park

Location: Ream National Park

Open: 8:00 AM – 6:00 PM

Uniqueness: Secluded coast backed by dense mangroves.

Otres Beach

Location: South of Sihanoukville

Open: 24/7

Uniqueness: Famous, laid-back bohemian atmosphere.

Ratanakiri

Wild Nature

Lakes, forests, waterfalls, and adventure.

Ratanakiri: The Wild Highland
For the traveler looking to go off the beaten path, Ratanakiri offers a rugged landscape of red dirt roads, ancient volcanic lakes, and dense primary rainforests. This "gem-stone" province is famous for the emerald Yeak Laom Lake and its rich indigenous culture.

Yeak Laom Lake

Location: 5km from Banlung

Open: 7:00 AM – 5:00 PM

Uniqueness: A 4,000-year-old volcanic crater lake.

Ka Chanh Waterfall

Location: 6km from Banlung

Open: 7:00 AM – 5:00 PM

Uniqueness: Surrounded by rubber plantations and suspension bridges.

Virachey National Park

Location: Border of Laos/Vietnam

Open: Best with a guide (Day trips)

Uniqueness: One of the largest protected wildernesses in Cambodia.

Kep

Coastal Charm

Fresh seafood, sea breeze, and peaceful views.

Kep: The Elegant Retreat
Kep is Cambodia's most tranquil seaside destination, known for its nostalgic charm and world-famous seafood. Originally founded as a prestigious retreat for the French elite, it offers a slower pace of life where the main "event" is choosing a fresh crab at the Crab Market.

Kep Beach

Location: Kep Town Center

Open: 24/7

Uniqueness: Calm crescent beach flanked by seafood shacks.

The Crab Market

Location: Waterfront, Kep

Open: 6:00 AM – 8:00 PM

Uniqueness: Watch live crabs pulled directly from the ocean.

Kep National Park

Location: Behind Kep Town

Open: Dawn to Dusk

Uniqueness: An 8km jungle loop with panoramic ocean views.

Kampot

Riverside Beauty

Mountains, pepper farms, and calm rivers.

Kampot: The Riverside Sanctuary
Nestled along the Praek Tuek Chhu River and framed by the misty Elephant Mountains, Kampot is famous for producing the world's best pepper. The town is a masterpiece of colonial architecture now filled with cozy cafes and boutique stays.

Bokor Hill Station

Location: Bokor National Park

Open: 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM

Uniqueness: An eerie, mist-covered French colonial ghost town.

Kampot Waterfall

Location: Tada/Popokvil area

Open: 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM

Uniqueness: Refreshing jungle pools perfect for a hot day.

Kampot Pepper Farm

Location: Rural Kampot

Open: 9:00 AM – 5:00 PM

Uniqueness: Certified "champagne of pepper" known worldwide.

Battambang

Cultural Heartbeat

Colonial charm, the unique Bamboo Train, and ancient cave formations.

Battambang: Cambodia's artistic sanctuary and the nation's legendary "Rice Bowl." Recently recognized as a UNESCO Creative City of Gastronomy, it is a paradise for foodies and culture seekers. From the quirky Bamboo Train to the haunting Bat Caves at sunset, Battambang provides a deep, authentic connection to Khmer history and modern creativity.

The Bamboo Train (Norry)

Location: 4km Southeast of city center

Open: 8:00 AM – 5:30 PM

Uniqueness: A DIY rail vehicle made of bamboo and small engines.

Phnom Sampeau (Bat Caves)

Location: 12km Southwest of the city

Open: 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM

Uniqueness: Millions of bats spiral out at sunset in a stunning display.

Phare Ponleu Selpak

Location: Northwest edge of the city

Open: Evening shows (~7:00 PM)

Uniqueness: A world-renowned NGO circus blending acrobatics, theater, and music.

Kampong Cham

Riverside Heritage

Scenic river views, unique bamboo architecture, and ancient hilltop temples.

Kampong Cham: Known as the "Gateway to the East," it features wide riverside boulevards, peeling French colonial buildings, and a warm atmosphere. Whether crossing a handcrafted bamboo bridge or exploring ancient temples, Kampong Cham offers an authentic slice of Cambodian life that feels timeless and serene.

Koh Paen Bamboo Bridge

Location: Mekong River bank, Kampong Cham Town

Open: 6:00 AM – 6:00 PM (Best Nov–May)

Uniqueness: Built by hand from bamboo poles — torn down and rebuilt every year!

Phnom Srey & Phnom Pros

Location: 7km from the provincial town

Open: 7:00 AM – 6:00 PM

Uniqueness: Two legendary hills from a myth about men vs women. (The women won!)

Wat Nokor Bachey

Location: 2.2km from the city center

Open: 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM

Uniqueness: A modern pagoda built inside 11th-century Angkorian ruins.

Preah Vihear

The Temple in the Clouds

Stunning cliff-top views, ancient stairways, and a unique north-south temple axis.

Preah Vihear: The crown jewel of Cambodia's northern highlands. Home to two UNESCO World Heritage sites — the cliff-top Preah Vihear Temple and the mysterious pyramid of Koh Ker — a testament to the architectural daring of the Khmer Empire.

Preah Vihear Temple

Location: Atop Poy Tadi Cliff, Dângrêk Mountains

Open: 7:00 AM – 5:00 PM

Uniqueness: UNESCO site perched 525m above the plains on a unique north-south axis.

Koh Ker Archaeological Site

Location: Srayang Commune, Kuleaen District

Open: 7:00 AM – 5:30 PM

Uniqueness: Home to Prasat Prang, a 35m-high seven-stepped pyramid.

Preah Khan of Kampong Svay

Location: Sangkum Thmei District

Open: 7:00 AM – 5:00 PM

Uniqueness: One of the largest temple complexes ever built during the Angkorian era.

Takeo

Cradle of Civilization

Ancient pre-Angkorian ruins, scenic mountain climbs, and a sprawling wildlife sanctuary.

Takeo: Widely celebrated as the "cradle of Khmer civilization," home to some of the country's oldest archaeological treasures dating back to the pre-Angkorian Funan and Chenla eras. A perfect spot for history lovers and weekend explorers alike.

Phnom Chisor

Location: 27km North of Takeo Town

Open: 7:00 AM – 5:00 PM

Uniqueness: An 11th-century mountain temple reachable by climbing 503 steps.

Phnom Tamao Wildlife Rescue Center

Location: Bati District (off National Road 2)

Open: 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM

Uniqueness: The largest zoo in Cambodia — sanctuary for rescued tigers, sun bears, and elephants.

Tonle Bati & Ta Prohm Temple

Location: 35km south of Phnom Penh, Bati District

Open: 7:00 AM – 6:00 PM

Uniqueness: A beautiful lake with over-water bamboo shacks next to a 12th-century Angkorian temple.

Banteay Meanchey

The Silent Sentinel

Massive stone face-towers, intricate bas-reliefs, and rare crane sanctuaries.

Banteay Meanchey: Cambodia's northwestern "Fortress of Victory," its true soul lies in the silent, moss-covered stones of Banteay Chhmar — a temple complex that rivals Angkor in beauty but without the crowds. A land of vast wetlands, golden rice fields, and ancient legends.

Banteay Chhmar Temple

Location: Thma Puok District (60km north of Sisophon)

Open: 7:30 AM – 5:30 PM

Uniqueness: Rare Bayon-style face-towers and a massive wall of multi-armed Avalokitesvara.

Ang Trapeang Thmor Sanctuary

Location: Phnom Srok District

Open: Best at dawn (5:30 AM – 8:00 AM)

Uniqueness: Critical wetland for the endangered Sarus Crane and rare Eld's Deer.

Phnom Bak & Phnom Chenh Chung

Location: Serei Saophoan City (Sisophon)

Open: 24/7 (Best for sunrise/sunset)

Uniqueness: Twin limestone mountains with a 360° view and a colorful hilltop pagoda.

Siem Reap

Fun Fact: "Siem Reap" literally means "Siam Defeated," commemorating a 16th-century Khmer victory over the Thai army. Home to the world's largest religious monument — Angkor Wat!

Phnom Penh

Fun Fact: Named after Lady Penh, who found four Buddha statues in a floating tree and built a hill (Phnom) and a temple (Wat Phnom) to house them.

Sihanoukville

Fun Fact: Built in the 1950s as Cambodia's only deep-sea port. Named after King Norodom Sihanouk, fondly remembered as the "Father of Independence."

Ratanakiri

Fun Fact: Ratna (Gem) + Kiri (Mountain) in Sanskrit — "Gem Mountain," famous for rare blue zircons and volcanic soil.

Kep

Fun Fact: The smallest province in Cambodia! Once the most prestigious beach resort for the French and Khmer elite — "The Saint-Tropez of Southeast Asia."

Kampot

Fun Fact: Kampot Pepper was the first Cambodian product to receive "Geographical Indication" (GI) status — the same protection as Champagne in France. Top chefs consider it the world's best pepper!

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before coming to Cambodia.

Do I need a visa to visit Cambodia?

Most travelers need a visa. ASEAN citizens can enter visa-free for 14–30 days. Everyone else can apply for an E-Visa online ($36) or get a Visa on Arrival ($30) at the airport.
Pro Tip: Complete your e-Arrival Card online within 7 days before you land!

What currency should I bring?

Cambodia uses a dual-currency system: US Dollars (USD) and Cambodian Riel (KHR). $1 ≈ 4,000 Riel. You pay in dollars and get small change back in Riel.
Pro Tip: Keep your US bills crisp and clean — banks may reject torn or old notes.

How do I get around the cities?

The easiest way is by Tuk-Tuk! Download Grab or PassApp for fixed prices without haggling. Most short city rides cost $1–$3.

Is there a dress code for the temples?

Yes! At sacred sites like Angkor Wat or the Royal Palace, you must cover your shoulders and knees. A shawl over a tank top may be rejected — wear a T-shirt and long pants or skirt to ensure entry.
